The problem

Most traders operate
without a real system.

Retail traders are expected to perform with discipline while working across spreadsheets, screenshots, notes apps, and broker dashboards that show P&L and nothing else. Your broker can tell you what you made. It can't tell you whether your execution is improving — or whether you keep making the same mistake in a different form.

01

No central record

Trade history lives across broker exports, screenshots saved to folders, and mental notes that fade. There is no single source of truth.

02

P&L without context

Standard dashboards show what you made or lost. They don't show what drove it, what you executed well, or what you keep repeating.

03

Review without structure

Most traders review sporadically, if at all. Without a system, patterns go unnoticed, mistakes compound, and progress stays invisible.

Who built this

Built from frustration,
not inspiration.

Daules started as spreadsheets and Notion templates that still didn't do what we needed. Logging trades manually worked. Seeing the actual picture — which setups were performing, where discipline was slipping, what mistakes were costing real money — required either significant manual work or software designed for institutional teams at institutional prices.

We built Daules because we needed it: a clean, focused journal and performance dashboard for retail traders who take the work seriously. No signals, no upsells, no bloat. Just the system we wished we'd had from the start.
